## P0 — Critical Issues

### 1. Homepage lacks extractable business definition

**Impact:** The homepage h1 is a brand tagline ("Tu familia merece claridad, no otro formulario."). When an AI assistant is asked "¿qué es TC Insurance?", it cannot reliably construct a definition from the homepage alone. The hero subtitle exists but is in a paragraph below the fold — some AI crawlers weight above-fold content more heavily.

**Fix:** Add a concise, factual entity definition within the first 200 characters of the visible homepage body, in a semantic element. Example: a visually subdued `<p>` or `<meta>` content that reads: "TC Insurance Agency Services, LLC es una agencia de seguros licenciada en Texas que ofrece seguros de salud, vida, gastos finales, dental, visión y Medicare para familias latinas en Dallas, Fort Worth y Lewisville."

### 2. No `AggregateRating` schema despite having 6 testimonials

**Impact:** Google AI Overviews and rich results for local businesses heavily favor businesses with structured rating data. Without it, TC Insurance cannot appear in comparison panels or "best insurance agents near me" AI summaries. Competitors with even one Google review in schema have an advantage.

**Fix:** Add `AggregateRating` to `LocalBusinessJsonLd.tsx` using the testimonials data (average: 4.83/5 across 6 reviews). See schema snippet in Schema Coverage section above.

### 3. License number absent from root structured data

**Impact:** When an AI is asked "¿está TC Insurance licenciada en Texas?", it must infer this from prose text on `/sobre-mi`. AIs reading the knowledge graph (schema) first will not confirm licensure. For insurance, regulatory trust signals are a top E-E-A-T factor.

**Fix:** Add `hasCredential` and `identifier` (NPN) fields to the `InsuranceAgency` entity in `LocalBusinessJsonLd.tsx` as shown in the Schema section above.

## P1 — Important

### 4. FAQ set is too process-focused, misses product-level AI queries

**Impact:** When users ask AI assistants "¿cuánto cuesta un seguro de salud en Texas?" or "¿qué cubre el seguro de gastos finales?", they expect direct answers. If the TC Insurance FAQ does not contain these questions, the AI will use a competitor's content instead.

**Fix:** Expand `src/data/faq.ts` with 8–10 product-level questions (see FAQ Gap Analysis section above). These will be rendered on the homepage and feed the `FAQPage` schema.

### 5. No pricing information anywhere

**Impact:** "¿Cuánto cuesta un seguro de vida?" is among the top insurance queries. Competitors who provide even a range ("desde $15/mes para gastos finales, $30-$150/mes para vida temporal") will be cited by AI over sites that say "contáctenos para más info." The Lewisville city page has one price mention ("desde $15/mes") but this is not in a canonical, schemed location.

**Fix:** Add a pricing FAQ entry and/or a pricing range table to each service detail page. Ranges are sufficient — exact quotes are not needed. This dramatically improves AI citability.

### 6. Statistics in blog posts lack source hyperlinks

**Impact:** AI systems evaluate citability by checking whether claims are sourced. The subsidios ACA post cites "34% average increase" and "4.1 million Texans enrolled" — these are excellent data points — but no source URL is linked in the content. Perplexity and similar AI search engines will not cite unsourced statistics.

**Fix:** For each statistic in blog posts, add a markdown hyperlink to the primary source (CMS, TDI, KFF, Census Bureau). E.g., `[4.1 millones de texanos inscritos](https://www.cms.gov/...)`.

### 7. Phone number has non-Texas area code (203 = Connecticut)

**Impact:** Local business AI signals depend partly on local phone numbers. A Texas insurance agency with area code 203 (Connecticut) creates a mismatch that may reduce confidence for AIs building local business knowledge. Google Business Profile and voice search also factor local number into proximity trust.

**Fix:** Consider acquiring a Texas-area phone number (214/972 for Dallas, 817 for Fort Worth, 469 for DFW) as the primary display number, keeping the current number as secondary.

## P2 — Improvements

### 8. `dateModified` is static on blog posts

Blog post schema sets `dateModified` equal to `datePublished`. For posts that are periodically updated (e.g., Medicare 2026 will need updates for 2027), this prevents AI recency signals.

**Fix:** Add an optional `updatedAt` field to the `BlogPost` interface in `blogPosts.ts` and use it for `dateModified` in the article schema when present.

### 9. No `@type: HowTo` for the advisory process

The 4-step process appears on multiple pages and in blog posts. This is exactly the type of content Google and AI systems feature in rich results.

**Fix:** Add HowTo schema on the homepage and Lewisville city page (see snippet in Schema Coverage section).

### 10. Legacy posts by "Thomas Castillo" remain in codebase

Four legacy posts (D&O, cyber risk, FINMA, international properties) have `status: "legacy"` and are filtered from listings.
