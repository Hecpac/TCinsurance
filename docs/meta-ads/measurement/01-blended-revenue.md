# Blended Revenue (Source of Truth)

Total revenue attributed to all channels combined. This is your north star metric.

## Why Blended Revenue

- No attribution model is perfect
- Meta over-reports, Google over-reports, organic gets under-reported
- Blended revenue answers the only question that matters: "Did total revenue go up?"

## How to Track

```
Blended Revenue = Total new policies written this month
Blended CPA = Total ad spend / Total new policies
Blended ROAS = Total revenue / Total ad spend
```

## TC Insurance Application

| Metric | Formula | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Policies this month | Count from CRM | 25 policies |
| Total ad spend | Meta + Google combined | $2,000 |
| Blended CPA | $2,000 / 25 | $80/policy |
| Revenue per policy | Avg first-year commission | $300 |
| Blended ROAS | ($300 x 25) / $2,000 | 3.75x |

## Rules

- Review monthly (not daily)
- Compare month over month, not day to day
- If blended revenue goes up when you increase Meta spend, Meta is working
- If blended revenue stays flat when you increase spend, you're wasting money
